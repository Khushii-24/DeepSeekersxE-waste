Tips for Choosing Green Electronics

Look for Certifications: Energy Star, EPEAT, RoHS

Consider Energy Consumption: Check power ratings and energy efficiency labels.

Evaluate Materials: Choose products made with recycled or renewable materials.

Prioritize Durability: Invest in well-built devices with longer lifespans.

Support Companies with Sustainable Practices: Research company environmental policies and initiatives.

Think about Repairability: Can the product be repaired? Are spare parts easily available?

Floppy Disk Pen Holder

Description: A cube-shaped pen holder made from four old floppy disks, giving a vintage tech look.

Components Used: Floppy disks, zip ties or hot glue.

Use: Organizes stationery items while reusing outdated floppy disks in a useful way.

Circuit Board Pendant Necklace

Description: A stylish pendant made from a small section of a circuit board attached to a metal chain.

Components Used: Small PCB piece, metal frame, chain, protective resin or clear coating.

Use: Creates a unique, upcycled fashion accessory from e-waste.

CD/DVD Lamp

Description: A stunning lamp that uses old CDs to create a light-diffusing effect. The reflective surfaces scatter light beautifully.

Components Used: Old CDs, a lamp stand, an LED bulb, screws or glue for attachment.

Use: Reuses CDs creatively to provide a visually appealing lighting solution.

Resistor Bracelet

Description: A handmade bracelet crafted using colorful resistors connected with wire and beads.

Components Used: Resistors, jewelry wire, beads, and clasps.

Use: Turns small electronic components into an elegant fashion piece.

Hard Drive Clock

Components Used: Hard drive platter, clock mechanism, small screws.

Use: Transforms outdated hard drives into functional and stylish timepieces.
